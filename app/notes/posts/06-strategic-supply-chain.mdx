### <a id ="1"> <b>1. Introduction, Supply Chain Basics and Challenges </b> </a>

Main supply-chain challenges in 2025 from all over the globe:
- <b>Geopolitical instability and trade fragmentation</b> _(conflicts_ Ukraine, Middle East, Taiwan tensions, Palestine; protectionism and tariffs)_
- <b>Demand volatility and inventory management </b> _(shifts in consumer behavior, oscillation between understocking and overstocking)_
- <b>Inflation</b>
- <b>Trasports and logistic problems</b> _(port congestion, limited container availability, driver shortage, rising fuel cost etc ...)_a
- <b>Digitalization and cyber security </b>
- <b>Restructuring</b> _(of all industries)_

Within a firm, there are several entities that play a role in the supply chain:
- <b>R&D</b> _(can improve internal supply chain, design product to optimize supply chain, design products to improge logistic [IKEA example], decide how often develop new products)_.
- <b>Finance</b> _(manage working capital, invoices and account payable/receivable, analyze tariffs and taxes that depend on supply chain, manage FX rate and finance hedging)_.
- <b>Operatoins</b> _(manage all the manufacturing and assemplying processes, key components of the supply chain management)_.

Between all the operators in the supply chain network, there's, in all directions, flow of:
- information
- materials
- money
- intellectual property

During supply chain management and evalutation, there are different factors to take in account:
- <b>Sourcing</b>
    - Sourcing buffer (inventory, timing, selection of supplies)
    - Workload leveling over time
- <b> Manufacturing </b>
    - Decoupling point
    - Asset utilization and saturation rate
    - Production cycle
    - Demand buffering (inventory, timing and excess capacity)
- <b> Delivery </b>

There are two important views to look at supply chain management:
- <b>The Cycle view </b>: each cycles occurs at the interface between to successive stages, it clearly defines processes and people involced, specifiying roles and responsibilities of each time. _According to this view, the customer cycle is the trigger for the whole chain._
$$
\text{Customer order} \to \text{Replenishment cycle } \to \text{Manufacturing cycle } \to {Purchasing cycle}
$$
- <b>The Pull/Push view</b>: processes belong to one of the following categories depending on the timing of their execution:
    - <b>Pull </b>: execution is started in response to a customer order _(REACTIVE)_
    - <b>Push </b>: execution is started in ancitipation _(SPECULATIVE)_
In this way, it's easier to idenfity the push-pull boundary, the decoupling point and the order penetration point.

<b> Modularization vs Postponmement</b>

<a href ="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/222558265_Evaluation_of_supply_chain_structures_through_modularization_and_postponement" target="_blanl"> Ernst & Kamrad framework </a> describes supply chain structures based on two levers:
- <b>Modularization</b> refers to the degree to which a product is composed by indipendent and stardadized parts ("modules"). _It concerns the relationship with suppliers, designers and materials._
- <b>Postponment </b> refers to the delaying and timing of the final differentiation of the product. _It concerns the timing of the assembly, the labelling, the logicist and the distribution._

Note that postponement can be done at different levels:
- <b>time postponement</b>: delaying the final assembly closest to the customer's order
- <b>form postponement</b>: delaying the product differentiation
- <b>place postponement </b>: delaying and separating the plant where the final assembly is done

Intermediaries outside the firm (wholesalelrs and distributors) can be used to facilitate the postponement.


<b>Example: Benetton's Sweater Postponement</b>
- Instead of dyeing cotton and yarn, Benetton makes the sweaters and postpones coloring the inventory.
- The company ships to the stores a limited quantity of sweaters to collect information about customers's tastes.
- The remaining un-colored sweaters is the dyed according to the collected data.

This results in:
- better reaction to customers's demands and trends
- delayed forecasts for specific colors
- ability to still use aggregated demand forecast to start manufacturing early
